                Vitamin D: Helps with bone growth and development, as well as immune function.
                Foods that are high in vitamin D include fatty fish like salmon, egg yolks, and
                fortified foods like milk, orange juice, and cereal.Vitamin D is a fat-soluble 
                vitamin that is essential for strong bones and teeth, as it helps the body absorb
                calcium and phosphorus. It is also important for immune function, muscle function, 
                and cell growth. The body can produce Vitamin D when the skin is exposed to sunlight,
                and it can also be obtained from food sources like fatty fish, egg yolks, and fortified 
                foods. A deficiency in Vitamin D can lead to bone loss, muscle weakness, and increased 
                risk of chronic diseases like osteoporosis and diabetes.
            

        

            

                Calcium: Important for bone and teeth health, as well as muscle and nerve function. 
                Foods that are high in calcium include milk, cheese, yogurt, fortified soy products, 
                and leafy green vegetables like spinach and kale.Calcium is an important mineral 
                for building strong bones and teeth, which is especially important during the 
                teenage years when bones are still growing. It also helps with muscle and nerve 
                function. You can get calcium from foods like milk, cheese, yogurt, and leafy 
                green vegetables. Make sure to include calcium-rich foods in your diet to keep 
                your bones and body healthy!
            

        

            

            B Vitamins: This group of vitamins includes B1, B2, B3, B6, B12, and folic acid, 
            and they are important for energy production, brain function, and red blood cell
            formation. Foods that are high in B vitamins include whole grains, meat, fish, 
            poultry, eggs, dairy products, leafy greens, and beans.B vitamins are a group 
            of vitamins that are important for energy production and brain function, which 
            is especially important during the teenage years when there are many demands on 
            the body and mind. They also play a role in red blood cell formation and maintaining 
            healthy skin, hair, and nails. You can get B vitamins from a variety of foods like 
            whole grains, meat, fish, poultry, eggs, dairy products, leafy greens, and beans. 
            Make sure to include these foods in your diet to stay energized and focused!
            
   
        

             

                Zinc: An important mineral for immune function and wound healing. 
                Foods that are high in zinc include meat, shellfish, legumes, nuts, 
                seeds, and dairy products.Zinc is a micronutrient that is required in 
                small amounts by the body, but it is still important for maintaining 
                good health.Zinc is an important component of many enzymes and proteins 
                in the body, and it helps to regulate various biological processes, such
                as DNA synthesis, cell division, and hormone regulation.Zinc is essential 
                for immune system function, as it helps to activate and regulate white 
                blood cells that fight off infections and diseases.Zinc is also important 
                for skin health, as it helps to promote wound healing and may even help 
                to prevent acne by regulating the production of sebum in the skin.
